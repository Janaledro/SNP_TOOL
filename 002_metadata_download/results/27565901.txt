Because of their shared epidemiological associations, we conducted pleiotropic analysis of genome-wide association studies of lung cancer (12 160 lung cancer case patients and 16 838 control subjects) and cardiovascular disease risk factors (blood lipids from 188 577 subjects, type 2 diabetes from 148 821 subjects, body mass index from 123 865 subjects, and smoking phenotypes from 74 053 subjects). We found that 6p22.1 (rs6904596, <em>ZNF184</em>) was associated with both lung cancer (<em>P</em> = 5.50x10<sup>-6</sup>) and blood triglycerides (<em>P</em> = 1.39x10<sup>-5</sup>). We replicated the association in 6097 lung cancer case patients and 204 657 control subjects (<em>P</em> = 2.40&#x02009;&#x000d7;&#x02009;10<sup>-4</sup>) and in 71 113 subjects with triglycerides data (<em>P</em> = .01). rs6904596 reached genome-wide significance in lung cancer meta-analysis (odds ratio = 1.15, 95% confidence interval = 1.10 to 1.21<strong>,</strong>
<em>P</em><sub>combined</sub> = 5.20x10<sup>-9</sup>). The large sample size provided by the lipid GWAS data and the shared genetic risk factors between the two traits contributed to the uncovering of a hitherto unidentified genetic locus for lung cancer.</p></div></div><div id="idm139625955130016" class="tsec sec headless whole_rhythm"><p id="__p3" class="p p-first">Genetic heritability of lung cancer is estimated to be 14% (<a href="#djw167-B1" rid="djw167-B1" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_583316945">1</a>), but only a few genetic risk loci have been identified to date in genome-wide association studies (GWAS) of lung cancer in Europeans (<a href="#djw167-B2" rid="djw167-B2" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_583316938">2</a>). Epidemiological studies have shown associations between lung cancer and cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk factors related to the metabolic syndrome (<a href="#djw167-B3" rid="djw167-B3" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_583316935">3</a>,<a href="#djw167-B4" rid="djw167-B4" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_583316940">4</a>). There is also substantial evidence that lipid metabolism and innate immunity evolved from common pathways, and consequently genes that influence lipid traits may also influence inflammation and subsequent cancer development (<a href="#djw167-B5" rid="djw167-B5" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_583316933">5&#x02013;7</a>). Lung cancer is also well known to be strongly associated with tobacco smoking. Predicated on the hypothesis that investigating shared genetic risk factors across these traits could enhance the possibility of identifying new genetic loci for lung cancer, we used quantile-quantile (Q-Q) plots (<a href="#djw167-B8" rid="djw167-B8" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_583316941">8</a>) (<a href="http://jnci.oxfordjournals.org/lookup/suppl/doi:10.1093/jnci/djw167/-/DC1" ref="reftype=extlink&amp;article-id=5241892&amp;issue-id=284673&amp;journal-id=753&amp;FROM=Article%7CBody&amp;TO=External%7CLink%7CURI&amp;rendering-type=normal" target="_blank">Supplementary Methods</a>, available online) to assess potential polygenic enrichment of single-nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) associated with lung cancer given association with each CVD risk factor or smoking phenotypes (<a href="/pmc/articles/PMC5241892/figure/djw167-F1/" target="figure" class="fig-table-link figpopup" rid-figpopup="djw167-F1" rid-ob="ob-djw167-F1" co-legend-rid="lgnd_djw167-F1"><span>Figure 1</span></a>; <a href="http://jnci.oxfordjournals.org/lookup/suppl/doi:10.1093/jnci/djw167/-/DC1" ref="reftype=extlink&amp;article-id=5241892&amp;issue-id=284673&amp;journal-id=753&amp;FROM=Article%7CBody&amp;TO=External%7CLink%7CURI&amp;rendering-type=normal" target="_blank">Supplementary Figure 1</a>, available online).

All statistical tests were two-sided unless otherwise specified. <em>P</em> values of less than .05 were considered statistically significant.</p><p id="__p6">The Q-Q plots show enrichment between lung cancer and LDL-C and between lung cancer and TG blood lipid traits across multiple <em>P</em> value thresholds, up to 10<sup>-5</sup> (<a href="/pmc/articles/PMC5241892/figure/djw167-F1/" target="figure" class="fig-table-link figpopup" rid-figpopup="djw167-F1" rid-ob="ob-djw167-F1" co-legend-rid="lgnd_djw167-F1"><span>Figure 1</span></a>, A and B), verified by an adaptive permutation procedure (<a href="http://jnci.oxfordjournals.org/lookup/suppl/doi:10.1093/jnci/djw167/-/DC1" ref="reftype=extlink&amp;article-id=5241892&amp;issue-id=284673&amp;journal-id=753&amp;FROM=Article%7CBody&amp;TO=External%7CLink%7CURI&amp;rendering-type=normal" target="_blank">Supplementary Table 3</a>, available online). It lies within a DNaseI hypersensitive region in small airway epithelial cells (SAEC) and A549 adenocarcinoma cells (ENCODE) and lies in a region hypomethylated in primary alveolar epithelial cells (AEC) from our laboratory (<a href="http://jnci.oxfordjournals.org/lookup/suppl/doi:10.1093/jnci/djw167/-/DC1" ref="reftype=extlink&amp;article-id=5241892&amp;issue-id=284673&amp;journal-id=753&amp;FROM=Article%7CBody&amp;TO=External%7CLink%7CURI&amp;rendering-type=normal" target="_blank">Supplementary Figure 7</a>, available online). rs7749305 alternate allele C appears to create ATF3 and HIF1A binding sites. Similar findings are evident in adipocytes (ENCODE), extending the pleiotropic association between lung cancer and lipid traits to their function in respective tissue types.
</p><p id="__p13">Additional functional analyses on relevant tissue types would be important to study how the genetic risk variants assert their downstream effect on the traits of interest.</p><p id="__p14" class="p">Our study emphasizes that pleiotropic analysis of GWAS data of epidemiologically related traits can uncover hitherto unidentified genetic associations. Moreover, some GWAS of quantitative traits may be much larger than disease-specific GWAS (like in the case of CVD risk factors vs lung cancer) and, thus, may improve the likelihood to identify new loci for the disease with the smaller sample size.</p></div><div id="__sec1" class="tsec sec"><h2 class="head no_bottom_margin" id="__sec1title">Funding</h2><p id="__p15" class="p p-first">This work was supported by the Intramural Research Program of the Division of Cancer Epidemiology and Genetics, National Cancer Institute, National Institutes of Health, DHHS, Bethesda, MD.</p><p id="__p16">Transdisciplinary Research for Cancer of Lung (TRICL): National Institute of Health
