Different whole blood RNA collection and processing methods are highly variable and might confound comparisons of results across studies. The main aim of the study was to compare genome-wide gene expression profiles obtained from the two widely used commercially available whole blood RNA collection systems - PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes. Comparisons of present call rates, variances, correlations and influence of globin reduction across the two collection systems was performed using <em>in vivo </em>glucocorticoid stimulation in 24 peripheral blood samples from three individuals.</p></div><div id="__sec2" class="sec"><h2>Results</h2><p id="__p2" class="p p-first-last">RNA quality, yield and numbers of detected transcripts from the two RNA collection systems was comparable, with no significant differences between the tube types. Globin reduction resulted in a significant increase in present call rates (<em>p </em>= 8.17 &#x000d7; 10<sup>-5 </sup>and <em>p </em>= 1.95 &#x000d7; 10<sup>-3 </sup>in PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes respectively) and significant decrease in gene expression variance in both RNA collection tubes (<em>p </em>= 0.0025 and <em>p </em>= 0.041 in PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes respectively). Comparisons of glucocorticoid receptor-stimulated gene expression profiles between the two collection tube systems revealed an overlap of only 17 to 54%, depending on the stringency level of the statistical thresholds. This overlap increased by 1-8% when the RNA samples were processed to remove the globin mRNA.</p></div><div id="__sec3" class="sec sec-last"><h2>Conclusion</h2><p id="__p3" class="p p-first-last">RNA obtained from PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes was comparable in terms of quality and yield, however, detectable gene expression changes after glucocorticoid receptor stimulation were distinct, with an overlap of only up to 46% between the two collection systems. This overlap increased to 54% when the samples were depleted of globin mRNA and drastically reduced to 17-18% when only gene expression differences with a fold change greater than 2.0 were assessed. These results indicate that gene expression profiles obtained from PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; differ drastically and should not be analyzed together. These data suggest that researchers must exert caution while interpreting expression profiles obtained through different RNA collection tubes.</p></div></div></div><div id="__sec4" class="tsec sec"><h2 class="head no_bottom_margin" id="__sec4title">Background</h2><p id="__p4" class="p p-first">Microarray gene expression profiling is a frequently used approach to identify susceptibility genes or biomarkers for a number of human diseases. Peripheral blood represents a target tissue not only for blood-borne diseases, but it is also an attractive surrogate tissue for other diseases, given that peripheral blood cells share more than 80% of the transcriptome with many tissues ranging from brain, colon, heart, kidney, lung, prostate to spleen and stomach [<a href="#B1" rid="B1" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150267">1</a>]. Whole blood is a valuable resource which is easily obtainable and from which RNA can be prepared economically with standardized extraction protocols. Genome-wide expression profiling of human blood samples however, faces numerous challenges. Robust detection of transcript levels and high reproducibility in conjunction with minimal experimental variance is essential to enable detection of true biological variance. While standardization of blood sample handling and processing procedures is essential for better comparisons of gene expression results across experiments, very few studies have investigated the influence of sample collection and its impact on whole blood transcriptome analysis.</p><p id="__p5">Two commercial systems are available for collection and immediate stabilization of peripheral blood RNA: the PAXgene&#x02122; Blood RNA Systems (PreAnalytiX) and Tempus&#x02122; Blood RNA Tubes (Applied Biosystems). These RNA collection systems use proprietary reagents which lyse blood cells immediately after collection in the tubes, stabilize the RNA and prevent induction of new transcripts. In addition to different RNA collection methods, there is also a current debate whether high amounts of globin mRNA, originating from immature reticulocytes reduces the sensitivity to detect differently expressed genes and subsequently adversely impact gene expression profiling using microarrays [<a href="#B2" rid="B2" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150273">2</a>]. While globin reduction appeared to be beneficial in terms of decreased variability in gene expression profiles and detection of small differences using Affymetrix arrays [<a href="#B3" rid="B3" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150272">3</a>], no increased sensitivity in the detection of transcriptional changes was observed using the Applied Biosystems Microarray System [<a href="#B4" rid="B4" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150277">4</a>]. A technical note also suggests no benefits of globin reduction when using Illumina BeadChip expression microarrays [<a href="#B5" rid="B5" class=" bibr popnode">5</a>]. Finally, RNA processing approaches in combination with globin reduction may produce distinct gene expression signatures which might not be directly comparable to expression profile from RNA without globin reduction [<a href="#B4" rid="B4" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150265">4</a>,<a href="#B6" rid="B6" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150268">6</a>].</p><p id="__p6" class="p p-last">To address the concerns about the reproducibility and robustness of peripheral blood RNA collection systems, we compared gene expression profiles from RNA samples collected in PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes using Illumina BeadChips microarrays. Since peripheral blood gene expression is known to be influenced by various factors including age, gender, subset of leucocytes, body mass index, and smoking, all of which increase the biological variability, we performed an <em>in vivo </em>challenge test to override or at least reduce these confounding factors. In previous studies glucocorticoids have been shown to induce up- or down-regulation of 100-1,000 genes in a cell-type specific way [<a href="#B7" rid="B7" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150281">7</a>] hence we used dexamethasone, a synthetic cortisol analogon, selective for the glucocorticoid receptor, to stimulate gene expression levels. The <em>in vivo </em>challenge test enabled comparisons between glucocorticoid receptor stimulated changes across the two different RNA processing methods.</p></div><div id="__sec5" class="tsec sec"><h2 class="head no_bottom_margin" id="__sec5title">Results</h2><div id="__sec6" class="sec sec-first"><h3>RNA yield and quality comparisons between PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes</h3><p id="__p7" class="p p-first-last">Differences between the two RNA collection systems were assessed at the level of total RNA yield, RNA quality measured by the spectrophotometer ratio and the RNA Integrity Number (RIN) was assessed using an Agilent 2,100 Bioanalyzer [<a href="#B8" rid="B8" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150283">8</a>]. High quality RNA was obtained from both PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes with average 260/280 ratios of 2.1 and an average RIN of 8.7 for PAXgene&#x02122; and 8.2 for Tempus&#x02122; tubes. Following globin reduction, there was a significant reduction in the RIN in both tubes (PAXgene&#x02122; average RIN - 8.2, p-value: 4.9 &#x000d7; 10<sup>-03 </sup>and Tempus&#x02122; average RIN - 7.1, p-value: 4.0 &#x000d7; 10<sup>-05</sup>). The total RNA yield obtained from the PAXgene&#x02122; samples was significantly lower than that obtained with Tempus&#x02122; tubes (2.04 &#x003bc;g/ml blood &#x000b1; 0.76 versus 2.98 &#x000b1; 0.91 &#x003bc;g/ml, <em>p </em>= 0.022). Globin reduction led to a loss of 47.3 &#x000b1; 14.7% in PAXgene&#x02122; tubes and of 27.0% &#x000b1; 7.8% of the RNA in Tempus&#x02122; tubes.</p></div><div id="__sec7" class="sec"><h3>Comparisons of baseline RNA expression between PAXgene&#x02122; and tempus&#x02122; tubes</h3><div id="__sec8" class="sec sec-first"><h4>Without globin reduction</h4><p id="__p8" class="p p-first-last">The number of significantly detected transcripts was determined using the Illumina probe detection threshold of <em>p </em>&#x02264; 0.01 in all samples within the group. There were no significant differences in the present call rates between the two RNA collection systems (<em>p </em>= 0.158). Overall, the mean number of transcripts detected above this threshold in PAXgene&#x02122; tubes was 8870 &#x000b1; 197 and in Tempus&#x02122; tubes was 9265 &#x000b1; 143. The average Pearson correlation between expression levels from the two systems was 0.94. No significant difference in the mean variance between expression profiles obtained from the PAXgene&#x02122; (3.03) versus Tempus&#x02122; (3.12) tubes was observed (<em>p </em>= 0.090). Baseline differences for two transcripts, TOMM7 and RAB37, whose expression levels were significantly different between the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes were validated using qPCR (Additional file <a href="#S1" rid="S1" class=" supplementary-material">1</a>: Figure S1).</p></div><div id="__sec9" class="sec sec-last"><h4>After globin reduction</h4><p id="__p9" class="p p-first-last">After globin-reduction, a significant increase in the number of probes with expression signals above detection threshold was observed in both PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes (Average number of significant detected genes in PAXgene&#x02122; tubes and Tempus&#x02122;: pre-globin reduction - 9762 &#x000b1; 972 and 9918 &#x000b1; 482, post-globin reduction - 11480 &#x000b1; 401 and 11608 &#x000b1; 247, <em>p </em>= 8.17 &#x000d7; 10<sup>-5 </sup>and <em>p </em>= 1.95 &#x000d7; 10<sup>-3 </sup>in PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes, respectively). An average correlation of R = 0.97 was observed for the 7,578 transcripts significantly detected in the pre- versus post-globin reduced samples. In accordance with previous reports [<a href="#B6" rid="B6" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150274">6</a>,<a href="#B9" rid="B9" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150275">9</a>], globin reduction resulted in a significant reduction in the variance (PAXgene&#x02122; tubes: mean variance of pre-globin reduced samples - 3.20, mean variance of post-globin reduced samples - 2.89, <em>p </em>= 0.0025 and Tempus&#x02122; tubes: mean variance of pre-globin reduced samples - 3.21, mean variance of post-globin reduced samples - 2.94, <em>p </em>= 0.041).</p></div></div><div id="__sec10" class="sec sec-last"><h3>Comparison of PAXgene&#x02122; RNA and tempus&#x02122; collection systems in combination with globin reduction on dexamethasone stimulated gene expression profiles</h3><p id="__p10" class="p p-first">After dexamethasone challenge 11107 &#x000b1; 930 transcripts were detected in PAXgene&#x02122; samples and 10135 &#x000b1; 1500 in Tempus&#x02122; specimens before globin reduction, with no significant increase in the present call rates (<em>p </em>= 0.25). To avoid a bias due to different detection rates between the two systems both before and after globin reduction and to ensure analysis of only robustly expressed transcripts, we analyzed 8,263 probes which were significantly detected in all 24 samples. At adjusted <em>p </em>&#x02264; 0.05, 449 transcripts and 365 transcripts were significantly regulated with fold changes of &#x02265; &#x000b1; 1.5 after dexamethasone stimulation in the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes, respectively (Figure <a href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F1/" target="figure" class="fig-table-link figpopup" rid-figpopup="F1" rid-ob="ob-F1" co-legend-rid="lgnd_F1"><span style="position: relative;text-decoration:none;">&#x200B;<span class="figpopup-sensitive-area" style="left: -3.5em;">(Figure1).</span></span><span>1</span></a>). An overlap of 100 transcripts between the two systems corresponding to a 46% overlap was observed. At adjusted <em>p </em>&#x02264; 0.01, 31 transcripts and 18 transcripts were significantly regulated with fold changes of &#x02265; &#x000b1; 2.0 after dexamethasone stimulation in the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes respectively (Figure <a href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F1/" target="figure" class="fig-table-link figpopup" rid-figpopup="F1" rid-ob="ob-F1" co-legend-rid="lgnd_F1"><span style="position: relative;text-decoration:none;">&#x200B;<span class="figpopup-sensitive-area" style="left: -3.5em;">(Figure1).</span></span><span>1</span></a>). An overlap of 7 transcripts between the two systems corresponding to a 17% overlap was observed.</p><!--fig ft0--><!--fig mode=article f1--><div class="fig iconblock whole_rhythm clearfix" id="F1" co-legend-rid="lgnd_F1"><a href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F1/" target="figure" rid-figpopup="F1" rid-ob="ob-F1"><!--fig/graphic|fig/alternatives/graphic mode="anchored" m1--><div data-largeobj="" data-largeobj-link-rid="largeobj_idm139823328642752" class="figure"><a class="inline_block ts_canvas" href="/core/lw/2.0/html/tileshop_pmc/tileshop_pmc_inline.html?title=Click%20on%20image%20to%20zoom&amp;p=PMC3&amp;id=3280191_1756-0500-5-1-1.jpg" target="tileshopwindow"><div class="ts_bar small" title="Click on image to zoom"></div><img alt="An external file that holds a picture, illustration, etc.&#10;Object name is 1756-0500-5-1-1.jpg" title="Click on image to zoom" class="tileshop" src="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/bin/1756-0500-5-1-1.jpg" /></a></div><div id="largeobj_idm139823328642752" class="largeobj-link align_right" style="display: none"><a target="object" href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F1/?report=objectonly">Open in a separate window</a></div></a><div class="icnblk_cntnt" id="lgnd_F1"><div><a class="figpopup" href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F1/" target="figure" rid-figpopup="F1" rid-ob="ob-F1">Figure 1</a></div><!--caption a7--><div class="caption"><p id="__p11"><strong>Venn diagram comparing PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tube gene expression: Comparisons based on dexamethasone stimulated gene expression before and after globin-reducing the RNA</strong>. No significant differences in the detection rates between the two RNA collection systems were observed. Expression profiles from the two tube types showed a Pearson correlation coefficient of 0.94, a value much lower than the usual Pearson correlation of 0.98-99 which is often observed for replicates [<a href="#B12" rid="B12" class=" bibr popnode">12</a>]. The mean variance in transcriptional levels obtained from Tempus&#x02122; tubes was higher than the variance in PAXgene&#x02122; tubes but this difference was not statistically significant. The finding that globin reduction significantly increased the sensitivity and significantly reduced the overall variance in gene expression profiles from both tubes was in line with previous studies reporting the positive impact of globin reduction on whole blood gene expression [<a href="#B6" rid="B6" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150282">6</a>,<a href="#B9" rid="B9" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150266">9</a>].</p><p id="__p17">Comparisons of the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes were performed using <em>in vivo </em>dexamethasone stimulation as a tool to assess the comparability of dynamic within- subject changes in gene expression between the two collection systems. Since setting the threshold of significance for statistical tests is an arbitrary measure, we set two thresholds which differed in the level of stringency. The threshold level of adjusted p &#x0003c; 0.05 and fold change of &#x02265; &#x000b1; 1.5 was a less stringent filter but probably closer to the threshold used in most microarray studies where usually only nominal fold changes are expected. The more stringent threshold of adjusted p &#x0003c; 0.01 and fold change of &#x02265; &#x000b1; 2.0 were chosen to select for highly robust changes in gene expression levels and to allow comparison of our results with that of Asare and colleagues [<a href="#B11" rid="B11" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150264">11</a>] who have also assessed the influence of RNA collection systems on gene expression profiles. Interestingly, despite using two statistical thresholds, the expression profiles obtained from PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes did not substantially overlap, with 46% overlap using the less stringent statistical threshold and the overlap being reduced to only 17% using the more stringent statistical threshold. Furthermore, we observed that the differences between the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes could not be overridden by globin-reduction of the samples. In fact, removal of globin-mRNA did not greatly increase the sensitivity to detect dexamethasone regulated transcripts. The overlap of 17% using the more stringent criteria showed a gain in sensitivity of only 1% over the non globin reduced samples. relative;text-decoration:none;">&#x200B;<span class="figpopup-sensitive-area" style="left: -3.5em;">(Figure2),</span></span><span>2</span></a>), there are several candidates such as FKBP5, IL18R1, IL1R1, ECHDC3, ZBTB16, PHC2 and TLR2 which we and others have found to be robustly induced by dexamethasone across independent datasets, thereby serving as positive controls [<a href="#B13" rid="B13" Matheson et al. reported that there were no significant differences between IL-2 levels between the two tubes which were shipped at two different temperatures [<a href="#B15" rid="B15" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150279">15</a>]. In another study, Asare and colleagues demonstrated that peripheral blood RNA isolation using the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes is significantly different and this critically impacts gene expression profiles obtained from the two RNA collection systems [<a href="#B11" rid="B11" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150263">11</a>]. In this study the average overlap between the two tubes for phytohemagglutinin (PHA) induced gene expression differences was 48% (at <em>p </em>&#x0003c; 0.01 and fold change &#x0003e; 2) when analyzed on the Affymetrix microarray system. In the current study we report a sizeable difference between the two tubes types with an overlap of only 17-18% on the Illumina microarray at the same statistical threshold. The discrepancy in the percentage of overlap might be partly explained by differences in the experimental set-up, type of stimulus to induce gene expression, microarray platforms and statistical procedures between the two studies.</p><p id="__p20" class="p p-last">The major disadvantage of this study is the small sample size and it would be interesting to replicate these findings independently in a larger dataset. The advantages of the study include a robust <em>in vivo </em>stimulation to detect dynamic differences in gene expression and qPCR validation of four transcripts in triplicates to demonstrate the technical reliability of the data. A further strength of this study is that the reported gene expression differences between the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes seem robust to RNA purification methods such as globin reduction. Also, to the best of our knowledge, this is the only study which compares the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes in the presence of globin reduction.</p></div><div id="__sec12" class="tsec sec"><h2 class="head no_bottom_margin" id="__sec12title">Conclusions</h2><p id="__p21" class="p p-first-last">Multicenter research studies involving collection and storage of large numbers of peripheral blood samples require a systematic approach for sample collection which is reliable and reproducible across different laboratories. Our results indicate that the choice of RNA collection tubes greatly influences the transcriptional profiles obtained from peripheral blood. While comparing transcripts differentially regulated after <em>in vivo </em>dexamethasone intake, we demonstrate a mere overlap of up to 54% between two of the most commonly used commercial systems for collection of peripheral blood RNA - PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes. We report that only transcripts which are differentially regulated with large, robust and significant fold changes are detected in both PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes. These results raise a very important question about the strong impact of the RNA isolation tubes on transcription levels which cannot be surpassed by downstream processes such as globin mRNA reduction and data analysis. These data suggest that combining gene expression profiles across the PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes is not advisable and researchers should use a single tube type throughout the project to avoid biases due to the RNA collection system. This study has great relevance for large clinical studies where whole blood gene expression profiles are analyzed for general research purposes and for identification of biomarkers for human diseases.</p></div><div id="__sec13" class="tsec sec"><h2 class="head no_bottom_margin" id="__sec13title">Methods</h2><div id="__sec14" class="sec sec-first"><h3>Study design and blood collection</h3><p id="__p22" class="p p-first">Three healthy male volunteers aged 30-35 years with body mass index of 20-25 were enrolled in the study (see Figure <a href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F3/" target="figure" class="fig-table-link figpopup" rid-figpopup="F3" rid-ob="ob-F3" co-legend-rid="lgnd_F3"><span style="position: relative;text-decoration:none;">&#x200B;<span class="figpopup-sensitive-area" style="left: -3em;">Figure3).</span></span><span>3</span></a>). All subjects were in good physical condition and free from medication. Leucocytes counts, C-reactive protein (CRP), gamma glutamyl transpeptidase (GGT), alanine aminotransferase (ALAT) and aspartate aminotransferase (ASAT) levels were not significantly elevated. The optimal time of blood collection for mRNA measurement after dexamethasone stimulation was defined in a pilot experiment. The t<sub>max </sub>of orally administered dexamethasone is about 1.5 h with a t<sub>1/2 </sub>of about 4 h [<a href="#B7" rid="B7" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150276">7</a>]. In our pilot experiment, maximal gene expression was observed 3 and 5 h after dexamethasone intake, hence we used a 3 h interval between blood sampling. Non-stimulated peripheral blood samples were obtained at noon after 2 h of fasting and abstention from coffee, smoking and physical activity, followed by orally administration of 1.5 mg dexamethasone. Stimulated peripheral blood samples were drawn 3 h later. For each sample, duplicate measures of 2.5 ml and 3 ml blood was collected in PAXgene&#x02122; tubes (PreAnalytiX GmbH, Hombrechtikon, Switzerland) and Tempus&#x02122; tubes (Applied Biosystems, Foster City, CA, USA) respectively. The PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes were stored for 2.5 h at room temperature and then transferred to -20&#x000b0;C. Due to the lower RNA yield from the PAXgene&#x02122; tubes, RNA from two PAXgene&#x02122; tubes was pooled together for the experiment.</p><!--fig ft0--><!--fig mode=article f1--><div class="fig iconblock whole_rhythm clearfix" id="F3" co-legend-rid="lgnd_F3"><a href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F3/" target="figure" rid-figpopup="F3" rid-ob="ob-F3"><!--fig/graphic|fig/alternatives/graphic mode="anchored" m1--><div data-largeobj="" data-largeobj-link-rid="largeobj_idm139823330191040" class="figure"><a class="inline_block ts_canvas" href="/core/lw/2.0/html/tileshop_pmc/tileshop_pmc_inline.html?title=Click%20on%20image%20to%20zoom&amp;p=PMC3&amp;id=3280191_1756-0500-5-1-3.jpg" target="tileshopwindow"><div class="ts_bar small" title="Click on image to zoom"></div><img alt="An external file that holds a picture, illustration, etc.&#10;Object name is 1756-0500-5-1-3.jpg" title="Click on image to zoom" class="tileshop" src="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/bin/1756-0500-5-1-3.jpg" /></a></div><div id="largeobj_idm139823330191040" class="largeobj-link align_right" style="display: none"><a target="object" href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F3/?report=objectonly">Open in a separate window</a></div></a><div class="icnblk_cntnt" id="lgnd_F3"><div><a class="figpopup" href="/pmc/articles/PMC3280191/figure/F3/" target="figure" rid-figpopup="F3" rid-ob="ob-F3">Figure 3</a></div><!--caption a7--><div class="caption"><p id="__p23"><strong>Flow diagram of the study design: Peripheral blood was drawn from 3 healthy young male volunteers at 12:00 and 3:00 pm using PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; collection tubes</strong>. After the first blood draw at baseline, volunteers were administered 1.5 mg dexamethasone orally and blood was collected again after 3 h. After blood collection, RNA (24 samples, 8 from each individual) was extracted using standard protocols and later processed either with or without globin reduction. Blood from 2 PAXgene&#x02122; tubes was pooled together to obtain enough RNA for the amplifications and the globin reduction procedure.</p></div></div></div><p id="__p24">Serum, EDTA and Li Herapin tubes (Sarstedt AG, N&#x000fc;mbrecht, Germany) were collected concurrently to analyse differential blood counts, cortisol and ACTH levels and liver enzymes. Administration of 1.5 mg dexamethasone p.o. resulted in a decrease in cortisol and ACTH levels at 3 h post-intake, indicating successful dexamethasone stimulation in all 3 volunteers.</p><p id="__p25" class="p p-last">The study was approved by the Ethics Committee of the Ludwig Maximilians University in Munich, Germany, and written informed consent was obtained from all subjects.</p></div><div id="__sec15" class="sec"><h3>RNA extraction, globin reduction and amplification</h3><p id="__p26" class="p p-first-last">Total RNA was isolated from whole blood stored in PAXgene&#x02122; and Tempus&#x02122; tubes according to the respective manufacturer's instructions. The PAXgene&#x02122; samples were processed using the PAXgene&#x02122; Blood RNA Kit based on the Quiagen method for column purification of nucleic acids (PreAnalytiX GmbH, Hombrechtikon, Switzerland, catalogue number 762174). The Tempus&#x02122; samples were isolated using the Tempus&#x02122; Spin RNA Isolation Kit (Applied Biosystems, Foster City, CA, USA, catalogue number 4380204). RNA quality was assessed using an Agilent 2100 Bioanalyzer (Agilent Technologies, Palo Alto, CA, USA). The concentration and purity of total RNA was independently assessed by 260/280 UV absorption ratios, respectively (Nanophotometer, Implen, Munich, Germany). RNA samples were divided into non-globin reduced and globin reduced groups. The GLOBINclear&#x02122;-Human Kit (Ambion, Inc., Texas, USA, catalogue number #AM1980) was used to remove globin mRNA. Sample amplification and labeling for both globin-reduced and non-reduced samples was performed using the Illumina<sup>R </sup>TotalPrep RNA Amplification Kit (Ambion, Inc., Texas, USA, catalogue number AMIL1791).</p></div><div id="__sec16" class="sec"><h3>Processing of samples on microarrays</h3><p id="__p27" class="p p-first-last">Biotin-labeled cRNA was hybridized to Illumina HumanHT-12 Expression BeadChips (Illumina, Inc., San Diego, CA, USA, catalogue number BD-103-0603) according to manufacturer's protocols. Microarrays were scanned with the Bead Array Reader (Illumina, Inc., San Diego, CA, USA).</p></div><div id="__sec17" class="sec"><h3>qPCR procedures</h3><p id="__p28" class="p p-first-last">For validation of the results, cDNA was synthesized from 500 ng total RNA using Superscript II Reverse Transcriptase (Invitrogen, Darmstadt, Germany). qPCR was performed using the Universal Probe Library for TOMM7, RAB37, FKBP5, IL18R1 and TBP on the Roche LightCycler 480 (Roche Applied Science, Penzberg, Germany). Assays were designed using the Probe Finder Software (Roche Applied Science) and run in triplicates in a total reaction volume of 10 &#x003bc;l.</p></div><div id="__sec18" class="sec sec-last"><h3>Statistical analysis</h3><p id="__p29" class="p p-first">Microarray expression analysis was performed in R [<a href="#B16" rid="B16" class=" bibr popnode">16</a>], using <em>beadarray </em>(providing routines to handle Illumina BeadStudio data) [<a href="#B17" rid="B17" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150269">17</a>], <em>limma </em>(for statistical routines) and <em>vsn </em>(for normalization) [<a href="#B18" rid="B18" class=" bibr popnode tag_hotlink tag_tooltip" id="__tag_270150280">18</a>] packages. Raw microarray scan files were exported using the Illumina Beadstudio program and loaded into R for downstream analysis. The data were transformed and normalized using the variance stabilizing normalization method. Of the 48804 probes present on the Human HT-12v3, probes were selected which fulfilled the criteria of Illumina probe detection p-value of &#x0003c; 0.01 in the individuals. Raw qPCR data from the Roche LightCycler 480 System was extracted and the crossing thresholds (C<sub>T</sub>) from the technical replicates were averaged across all samples. Transcripts were normalized to housekeeping gene TBP.</p><p id="__p30" class="p p-last">Significantly regulated genes were detected using generalized linear models and corresponding fold changes between the groups in R. For differential expression analysis, functions from the limma package [<a href="#B19" rid="B19" class=" bibr popnode">19</a>] were used on the normalized log<sub>2</sub>-transformed expression values before and after dexamethasone challenge; group differences were tested for collection method, globin reduction status, and pre- vs. post dexamethasone stimulation. Results were corrected for multiple testing by 10,000 permutations for each transcript using the permutation of regressor residuals test (PRR) as implemented in the R package glmperm (<a href="http://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/glmperm/index.html" ref="reftype=extlink&amp;article-id=3280191&amp;issue-id=204777&amp;journal-id=634&amp;FROM=Article%7CBody&amp;TO=External%7CLink%7CURI&amp;rendering-type=normal" target="_blank">http://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/glmperm/index.html</a>). Clinical variables and RNA quality measures were compared using a one way ANOVA with SigmaStat<sup>&#x000ae; </sup>for Windows (Release 2.0, SPSS Inc., Chicago, Illinois 60606, USA).</p></div></div><div id="__sec19" class="tsec sec"><h2 class="head no_bottom_margin" id="__sec19title">Abbreviations</h2><p id="__p31" class="p p-first-last">RIN: RNA integrity number; CRP: C-reactive protein; GGT: Gamma glutamyl transpeptidase; ALAT: Alanine aminotransferase; ASAT: Aspartate aminotransferase.</p></div><div id="__sec20" class="tsec sec"><h2 class="head no_bottom_margin" id="__sec20title">Competing interests</h2><p id="__p32" class="p p-first-last">The authors declare that they have no competing interests.</p></div><div id="__sec21" class="tsec sec"><h2 class="head no_bottom_margin" id="__sec21title">Authors' contributions</h2><p id="__p33" class="p p-first-last">AM, EBB and DM participated in the design of the study and drafted the manuscript.
